My Teaching Philosophy

"It's got to be engaging."

Learning requires students to actively engage during lectures and develop skills through lots of practice and solving application problems. It is essential to provide fun and interactive learning experiences through different styles of teaching methods by incorporating the utilization of technology in enhancing student learning.

I encourage peer learning among students during lectures because I believe that peer learning is the best method to improve students' academic performance. This is because peers can provide a great influence on each other's activities. I motivate them to solve the problem independently at the end of the lecture by shifting more responsibility to students for their learning instead of one-way teaching.

"Learning happens when you trap a student in an environment where they can't escape without thinking."

💡 Innovations & Creativity

"The important thing for you is to be alert, to question, to find out, so that your own initiative may be awakened." — Bruce Lee

Active and Collaborative Learning: Game-Based Challenge

Creating a dynamic environment that can inspire students to develop skills and competencies as they focus on the activities of the game.

  • Students play the game in groups. Each group must have a whiteboard to write down the solutions.
  • Questions appear one by one on the screen within the set time limit.
  • Students need to answer the questions on the board instantly.
  • Marks are given based on the correct answer and the fastest.
  • Prizes are waiting for the best three groups which collect the highest marks.
Game-Based Learning Teamwork Competition Active Participation

Cooperative Learning: Jigsaw Puzzles

Jigsaw puzzle is one of the cooperative learning activities to increase positive educational outcomes. Students work independently among themselves and self-learning is driven.

  • Divide students into 5-person jigsaw groups. The day's lesson is divided into 5 topics.
  • Form temporary "expert groups" by having one student from each jigsaw group join other students assigned to discuss the main points of their topic.
  • Bring the students back into their jigsaw groups and they have to present the topic to the group.
  • At the end of the session, students are given a question to solve in their jigsaw groups.
Jigsaw Method Cooperative Learning Peer Teaching Self-Learning

🔬 Program of Research

The central research inquiry for my program of research in Information Science and Digital Literacy is:

How can we advance digital competencies, AI literacy, and sustainable computing practices to enhance organisational performance and societal well-being?

I address this question through three interconnected strategies:

Human-Centred Digital Transformation
Understanding how people interact with technology to design inclusive and effective digital systems.
AI Literacy & Competency Frameworks
Developing frameworks to measure and enhance AI literacy in education and the public sector.
Sustainable Computing & Green IT
Promoting environmentally responsible computing through virtualization and end-user practices.
